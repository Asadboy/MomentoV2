# 10shots — App Store Screenshot Copy & Narrative

iPhone 6.9″ (1320×2868). 6 slides. Dark brand (royal-purple/blue glow on near-black).

## ASO note
App Store screenshots are **not** keyword-indexed — the App Store *name, subtitle, and
keyword field* carry literal search terms (see `APP_STORE_COPY.md`). Screenshots optimise
**tap-through from search results**, where slides 1–3 appear. So: slide 1 also carries the
searched *concept* ("disposable camera for your event") for the skimmer, while every
headline stays one-idea / 3–5 words / readable in a second.

## Why these (per-slide rationale)

1. **Hero** — embodies the app name; states the whole promise in 4 words. The sub-line is
   the only place we spend words on the searched concept ("disposable camera"), because
   this is the one slide every search browser sees.
2. **The catch** — names the unique constraint that makes 10shots not-Instagram. Kills the
   "everyone curates" pain. Contrast-light treatment for rhythm.
3. **Together** — the social mechanic; "same night" = time-bound + shared.
4. **The reveal** — the emotional peak/outcome. The dot-hero pays off here.
5. **So easy** — removes the friction objection (how do friends join?).
6. **CTA** — identity + call to action; inverted background closes the set.

## Layout treatment (anti-templating)
- S1 centred device, bottom. S2 device-right + caption-left. S3 device-left, tilted.
- S4 centred, larger (the payoff). S5 device-right. S6 no device — icon + wordmark, inverted.
- 1–2 contrast (light) slides among the dark set for visual rhythm (S2, S6).
