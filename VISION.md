# 10shots — Vision & Product Direction

**Status:** Pre-launch
**Last updated:** May 2026

---

## One-Liner

> Everyone at the party gets 10 shots. The next morning, the whole roll is revealed.

---

## Product

10shots is a **shared disposable camera for parties**. Every guest gets a roll of exactly 10 shots, photos stay locked until the event ends, and everyone sees the full collection together when the reveal lands.

It exists to bring back the feeling of a disposable camera in a world of infinite camera rolls. Not every moment needs to be captured — only the ones worth spending a shot on.

The experience has two halves:

- **During the event** — the constraint of 10 shots makes every photo deliberate. You're not documenting; you're choosing.
- **After the reveal** — flipping through the roll is the product. Laughing at the unexpected ones, saving the beautiful ones, seeing what your friends saw.

The reveal is the delivery mechanism. The feeling is the product.

---

## Audience

### The host

The host is the centre of the loop. It's their birthday, their flat, their night. 10shots gives them:

- A fun thing to do at the party ("everyone download 10shots tonight")
- A sentimental record of how their friends saw the night
- Content to post the next morning

The host does the legwork. Every successful event puts the app in front of 5–15 future hosts.

### The guest

Guests need it to be:

- Effortless to join (code or QR)
- Fun to use during the event (10-shot mechanic visible at all times)
- Rewarding the next morning (the reveal does the work)

If a guest has a great experience, they become a future host.

### Ideal events

Birthdays, house parties, small gatherings of 5–15 people who know each other. Not raves, not large public events, not professional settings.

---

## Core Mechanics

### 10 shots. No exceptions.

Every guest gets **10 shots** per event. This is the entire pivot.

- No bonus shots
- No earning more
- Join late? Still 10
- Host? Also 10
- Run out? You're done

The constraint is the feature. It produces intentionality, quality, equal participation, and a curated story per guest.

### The shot counter

Remaining shots must be **painfully obvious** at all times in the camera UI. The countdown from 10 to 0 is the signature of the experience — not a subtle UI element.

### The reveal

- Photos are locked from the moment they're taken until the event ends
- After the event ends there's a short gap before reveal time, shown as a "Reveals in X" countdown
- A push notification tells guests when the reveal is ready
- The reveal is currently solo — each guest scrolls the full roll on their own time
- During reveal you can like or skip; liked photos go to your gallery

### The done pile

Once an event is past its reveal time and you've completed the reveal, it lives in the **done pile** — a list of past events with stats (shots taken, likes, people). Tapping opens the liked gallery for that event. No re-reveal.

## Brand

### What 10shots is

- **Authentic** — real photos, no retakes, no filters beyond the disposable look
- **Fun** — a party feature, not a productivity tool
- **Disposable** — impermanent in feeling, precious because of it
- **Simple** — one mechanic, no complexity

### What 10shots is not

- Not a social network — no feed, no followers, no public profiles
- Not a photo editor — no filter packs, no beautification
- Not a messaging app — no comments, no DMs
- Not a content factory — it captures moments, it doesn't produce them

### Anti-features

Things deliberately not built:

- Likes/reactions visible publicly (likes are private to the liker, used to filter the gallery)
- Social feeds, explore pages, public profiles, follower counts
- Photo editing, beautification, retouching
- Comments on photos

---

## Product Principles

1. **The constraint is the feature.** 10 shots is the entire point. Never dilute it.
2. **The host is the hero.** Every product decision should make the host look good.
3. **Participation over perfection.** A blurry photo from a friend beats a perfect photo from a stranger.
4. **Don't fight for attention.** 10shots opens at parties and the morning after. That's it. No engagement tricks.
5. **Ship, then polish.** First 100 users are friends. Watermarks, aesthetics, premium ideas all wait.

---

## Monetisation

**v1 ships free.** No premium tier, no IAP, no paywalls. Premium event features were scoped and scrapped — the priority is validating the core mechanic with friends-of-friends.

Future revenue paths to revisit only after product-market fit:

- Extra rolls — host buys additional rolls of 10 for guests
- Print-on-demand — favourite shots delivered as physical prints
- Permanence — pay to keep photos beyond a future retention window

The host is the eventual paying customer. Guests should never hit a paywall.

## Growth

### The loop

```
Host creates event
  → 5–15 guests join
    → Guests have a great experience
      → Guests host their own event later
```

Target: every host eventually generates at least one future host.

### Channels

- **Word of mouth** — "we used this app at the party last night" is the primary acquisition channel
- **Post-reveal sharing** — guests share favourite shots to group chats and stories

Not in the strategy: paid acquisition, daily-engagement loops, public discovery, social-feed mechanics.

---

## v1 Scope (Ship Now)

- 10 shots per guest, hard limit
- Prominent shot counter in the camera UI
- Host creates event with start, end, and reveal times
- Guests join via 6-character code or QR
- Photos locked until reveal
- Reveal experience: swipe through, like or skip
- Liked gallery + done pile after reveal
- Save shots to Photos, share via system share sheet
- Disposable-style filter applied at capture

**Explicitly not in v1:**

- Web album / shareable web link (future, behind a real domain)
- Premium tier / IAP
- Photo retention policy
- Cross-event social features
- Comments
- Print-on-demand

---

## Differentiation

> Why not just use a shared iCloud album?

Because 10shots isn't about collecting photos — it's the experience of taking them under a constraint and the chaos of seeing them together the next morning. A shared album is a folder. 10shots is a disposable camera that the whole party shares.

The moat is the **mechanic plus the emotion**, not the technology.

## Metrics That Matter

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Shots used per guest | 7+ average |
| Participation rate (% guests who take ≥1 shot) | 80%+ |
| Reveal open rate | 90%+ |
| Guest-to-host conversion | This is the growth metric |
| Shots shared externally | Organic-reach proxy |
| Time to first shot after joining | Lower is better |
